Chandrasekhara Swamy Kalyanam

Shivaratri Kalyanam is a significant ritual performed at the Sri Chandrasekhara Kalikamma Temple, held annually on the auspicious night of Maha Shivaratri. This sacred ceremony symbolizes the divine wedding of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati, attracting numerous devotees who come to witness and partake in the celebrations. The temple is beautifully adorned with flowers and lights, creating a vibrant and spiritual atmosphere. Devotees engage in various rituals, including abhishekam, where the deity is bathed with holy substances such as milk, honey, and ghee, followed by the chanting of Vedic mantras and hymns. The Kalyanam is marked by a grand procession, where the idols of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati are taken around the temple premises, accompanied by the rhythmic beats of traditional music and dance. This event not only holds immense religious significance but also fosters a sense of community and devotion among the worshippers, making it a memorable and spiritually enriching experience for all.



Amavasya Hanuman Pooja

Amavasya Hanuman Pooja is a revered ritual performed at the Sri Chandrasekhara Kalikamma Temple every Amavasya (New Moon day). Devotees gather to seek the blessings of Lord Hanuman, revered for his unwavering devotion and immense strength. The pooja begins with a serene abhishekam, where the deity is ceremoniously bathed with sacred substances like milk, curd, honey, and holy water, symbolizing purification and sanctity. Unlike typical Hanuman poojas, this ritual is unique as it is conducted without the chanting of Hanuman Chalisa, focusing instead on other powerful mantras and hymns. Devotees offer garlands, fruits, and sweets, and perform aarti, creating an atmosphere filled with devotion and spiritual fervor. The ritual serves as a moment of deep spiritual connection, where devotees pray for strength, protection, and the fulfillment of their wishes. The Amavasya Hanuman Pooja at Sri Chandrasekhara Kalikamma Temple is a spiritually enriching experience that fosters faith and communal harmony among the worshippers.



Kaalikamma Varalakshmi Vratham

Kaalikamma Varalakshmi Vratham is a revered ritual performed at Sri Chandrasekhara Swamy Temple, celebrated with great devotion and fervor. This auspicious event is dedicated to Goddess Lakshmi, the harbinger of prosperity and well-being. The vratham, observed mainly by women, takes place on the Friday before the full moon in the month of Shravana. Devotees prepare by purifying themselves and setting up a beautifully adorned altar with an idol or picture of Goddess Lakshmi, embellished with flowers, turmeric, kumkum, and various offerings such as fruits, sweets, and traditional dishes. The ritual involves the recitation of the Varalakshmi Vratham katha (story), singing hymns, and chanting mantras in praise of the Goddess. At Sri Chandrasekhara Swamy Temple, the celebration includes elaborate decorations, special prayers, and collective chanting, creating a spiritually charged atmosphere. The temple priests conduct abhishekam and aarti, invoking the divine presence of Goddess Lakshmi and seeking her blessings for the devotees. This vratham is not only a profound expression of faith and devotion but also a joyous occasion that brings the community together in celebration of divine grace and prosperity.



Dasara Navaratri Ammavari Brahmotsavalu

Dasara Navaratri Ammavari Brahmotsavalu is a grand and vibrant festival celebrated at Sri Chandrasekhara Kalikamma Temple with immense devotion and enthusiasm. This nine-day festival, dedicated to Goddess Durga, marks the triumph of good over evil and is observed with elaborate rituals and ceremonies. The temple is beautifully decorated with lights, flowers, and traditional rangoli, creating a festive and spiritual ambiance. Each day of Navaratri is significant, with special poojas, abhishekam, and aarti performed to honor different forms of the Goddess. Devotees participate in chanting hymns, singing devotional songs, and offering prasad to seek the blessings of the Goddess. The highlight of the festival is the Brahmotsavam, where the idol of the Goddess is taken in a grand procession around the temple, accompanied by music, dance, and the chanting of Vedic mantras. This procession symbolizes the divine presence and blessings of the Goddess, spreading joy and spiritual energy among the devotees. The Dasara Navaratri Ammavari Brahmotsavalu at Sri Chandrasekhara Kalikamma Temple is a spiritually enriching and culturally vibrant celebration, fostering a deep sense of devotion and community spirit among the worshippers.



Shivvaya Karthika Pournami

Shivayya Karthika Pournami is a significant and spiritually enriching festival celebrated at Sri Chandrasekhara Kalikamma Temple. Held on the full moon day of the Karthika month, this event is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is marked by various rituals and ceremonies. Devotees gather in large numbers to participate in the festivities, which begin with a special abhishekam, where the Shiva Linga is bathed with holy substances such as milk, honey, and ghee. The temple is beautifully decorated with oil lamps, flowers, and traditional rangoli, creating a divine and serene atmosphere. Chanting of Vedic hymns, singing of devotional songs, and performing aarti are integral parts of the celebration, invoking the blessings of Lord Shiva for health, prosperity, and spiritual growth. The highlight of Karthika Pournami is the lighting of thousands of lamps, symbolizing the dispelling of darkness and ignorance, and the victory of light and knowledge. The Shivayya Karthika Pournami at Sri Chandrasekhara Kalikamma Temple is a deeply revered and joyous occasion, fostering a sense of community and spiritual devotion among the devotees.



Aashadamasa Ammavari Maha Mangala Pooja

Ashadamasa Ammavari Maha Mangala Pooja is a deeply revered ritual performed at Sri Chandrasekhara Kalikamma Temple during the auspicious month of Ashada. This special pooja is dedicated to the Goddess, symbolizing prosperity, health, and divine blessings. Devotees gather in large numbers to participate in the ceremony, which includes elaborate rituals such as abhishekam, where the deity is bathed with sacred substances like milk, honey, and ghee, followed by the adornment with flowers, turmeric, and kumkum. The temple is beautifully decorated, enhancing the spiritual ambiance and drawing devotees into a state of deep devotion and reverence. Chanting of hymns, recitation of Vedic mantras, and singing of devotional songs are integral parts of the pooja, creating a spiritually charged atmosphere. The Maha Mangala Aarti, a highlight of the ceremony, is performed with great devotion, invoking the Goddess's blessings for the well-being and prosperity of the devotees. The Ashadamasa Ammavari Maha Mangala Pooja at Sri Chandrasekhara Kalikamma Temple is a spiritually uplifting event, fostering a sense of community and deepening the devotees' connection with the divine.



Hanuman Jayanthi

Hanuman Jayanthi is a significant festival celebrated at Sri Chandrasekhara Kalikamma Temple, marking the birth of Lord Hanuman, the embodiment of devotion, strength, and courage. Devotees flock to the temple to honor Hanuman, who is revered for his unwavering devotion to Lord Rama and his immense power. The celebrations begin with an abhishekam, where the idol of Hanuman is ceremoniously bathed with holy substances such as milk, curd, honey, and ghee. The temple is adorned with vibrant decorations, creating an atmosphere of joy and reverence. Devotees chant Hanuman Chalisa and other hymns, offering flowers, fruits, and sweets to the deity. The pooja includes special rituals and aarti, invoking Hanuman's blessings for strength, protection, and the fulfillment of wishes. The highlight of the celebration is the Pallaki Seva, where the idol of Hanuman is taken in a grand procession around the temple, accompanied by music and dance, symbolizing his divine presence and blessings. Hanuman Jayanthi at Sri Chandrasekhara Kalikamma Temple is a spiritually enriching and joyous occasion, bringing the community together in a collective expression of faith and devotion.
